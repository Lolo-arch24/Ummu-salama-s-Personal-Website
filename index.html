About

Hello! I'm Ummu salama, a highschool senior based in Kano, Nigeria who absolutely LOVES indulging in anything related to books, crochet and coding! There's not much to know about me besides that. I've lived a pretty basic and eventless life; been living in the same house since I was born and going to the same school almost all my life with pretty much the same friends. Like I said... eventless

I've always been the type of person that loves trying new things and that resulted in me having a lot of hobbies. I've been in love with books for as long as I can remember, reading has always been my favourite thing to do wherever I was. Gaming is also one of the hobbies that I've had for as long as I can remember, I loved games so much that I decided that I wanted to become a game developer. I've played many games over the years, some of the ones that really stood out to me are; An elmwood trail, 7 years from now, Sky: children of the light and But you seem fine. My friends don't really play games so it gets kind of lonely sometimes.

I picked up crochet a little down the line, almost all the girls in my school used to crochet back then, very few were actually good at it though, and I was definitely not among them lol. But I enjoy it a lot, it makes me happy when I make something from scratch with my hands. I made the impulsive desicion to start learning a language almost three years ago, started with Japanese and I've now started learnng French too. To be honest I can't even remember why I started learning a new language or why I chose Japanese, but it has also become one of the activities that make me feel happy and good about myself. Learning programming has been a huge roller coaster with lots of ups and downs, I heard someone say "the thing with programming is that you have to love it more than you hate it, because you will hate it" I didn't quite understand what they meant until I started learning. There were many times where I was tempted to give up but just couldn't let go of it yet. The joy and satisfaction that comes when everything goes right is not something I plan on giving up anytime soon.
